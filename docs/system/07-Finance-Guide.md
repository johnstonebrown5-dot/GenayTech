# EDU-TRACK — Finance Guide

This guide is for finance/accounts staff using EDU-TRACK to manage fees, balances, statements, and finance reporting.

## 1. Getting started

### Step 1: Sign in

- Open the EDU-TRACK web address provided by your school.
- Enter your username/email and password.
- If you cannot sign in, request assistance from the administrator.

### Step 2: Confirm finance access

- Confirm you can access finance pages (fees, balances, statements).
- If you can sign in but cannot see finance pages, request the Finance role.

## 2. Fee setup (first-time or start of term)

### Step 1: Confirm the active term

- Confirm the current term/semester is correct before applying charges.

### Step 2: Confirm fee items

- Ensure fee items exist (e.g., Tuition, Lunch, Transport).
- If an item is missing, request the administrator to create it (or create it if your role allows).

### Step 3: Create/update fee structure

- For each class/grade, confirm the term fee structure.
- Confirm whether the structure is:

  - term-based (recommended for many schools), or
  - annual-based split across terms.

### Step 4: Apply charges to students (posting)

- Post/apply charges for the selected term.
- Verify the number of learners affected matches the class list.

Good practice:

- apply charges only once per term
- keep a record of any changes made mid-term (who approved and why)

## 3. Recording payments

### Step 1: Receive payment

- Confirm payment reference/receipt details from the payer.
- Confirm the student (use admission number where possible).

### Step 2: Record payment in EDU-TRACK

- Select the student.
- Enter the amount paid and payment date.
- Enter a payment reference (receipt number, transaction ID).

### Step 3: Confirm updated balance

- Verify the student’s balance updates correctly.
- If the balance does not change, re-check:

  - you selected the correct student
  - the payment amount and date
  - the payment was saved successfully

## 4. Managing balances and arrears

### Step 1: View balances

- Use finance dashboards/reports to view balances by class, stream, or term.

### Step 2: Identify arrears

- Filter to show students with outstanding balances.
- Prepare follow-up lists for class teachers/administration.

### Step 3: Handle corrections

Common correction scenarios:

- wrong student selected
- wrong amount entered
- duplicate payment entry

In these cases:

- correct or reverse the transaction according to school policy
- record the reason for correction (audit trail)

## 5. Generating statements

### Step 1: Generate an individual statement

- Open the student profile.
- Choose statement option (print/email) where available.
- Verify the statement has:

  - student details
  - term balances
  - payments received
  - opening/closing balances

### Step 2: Generate bulk statements (if enabled)

- Select class/stream.
- Generate statements in bulk.
- Validate a small sample before distributing widely.

## 6. SMS/email reminders (optional)

### Step 1: Confirm messaging is enabled

- Confirm SMS/email settings have been configured.
- Confirm the school has sufficient SMS credit (if using Africa’s Talking).

### Step 2: Send reminders

- Use approved message templates.
- Avoid including sensitive personal data in SMS.

## 7. Reconciliation and end-of-day checks

### Step 1: Daily reconciliation

- Compare collected amounts (cash/bank/mobile) with recorded transactions.
- Investigate discrepancies immediately.

### Step 2: End-of-term reconciliation

- Confirm fee structures, charges, and payments for the term are complete.
- Export key reports if required.

## 8. Reports and exports

Common finance outputs include:

- fee collection summary
- arrears list by class
- payment register by date range
- statement exports

When exporting:

- confirm the date range and term
- protect exported files (they contain personal/financial data)

## 9. Security and privacy

- Do not share finance logins.
- Restrict who can view balances and download statements.
- Store printed statements securely.

## 10. Getting help (support and escalation)

If you encounter errors, provide the administrator with:

- what you were doing
- the exact error message
- time of occurrence
- affected student(s) and payment reference(s) (where relevant)

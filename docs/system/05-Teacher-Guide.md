# EDU-TRACK — Teacher Guide

This guide is for teachers using EDU-TRACK for daily attendance and academic records.

## 1. Getting started

### Step 1: Sign in

- Open the EDU-TRACK web address provided by your school.
- Enter your username/email and password.

### Step 2: Confirm the active term and class/stream

- Before entering attendance or marks, confirm you are working in the correct term.
- If the class list looks wrong, notify the administrator before proceeding.

## 2. View class list

### Step 1: Open your class/stream

- Navigate to your assigned class/stream.
- Confirm learner names and admission numbers.

### Step 2: Handle missing/duplicate learners

- If a learner is missing, request the administrator to enroll/assign the learner.
- If a learner appears twice, report immediately to avoid double-records.

## 3. Take attendance (daily)

### Step 1: Open the attendance page

- Select the correct date (usually today).
- Select the correct class/stream.

### Step 2: Mark presence/absence

- Mark each learner as present or absent according to your school policy.
- Add notes/reasons (optional) where the system supports it.

### Step 3: Save and confirm

- Save attendance.
- Confirm totals (present/absent) look correct.

Good practice:

- take attendance early in the day
- avoid editing past dates unless you are correcting a confirmed mistake

## 4. Enter assessments / marks

### Step 1: Confirm assessment context

- Confirm the correct term.
- Confirm the subject.
- Confirm the assessment type/category (CAT, Midterm, Endterm) where applicable.

### Step 2: Enter marks

- Enter marks per learner.
- Save frequently if the system allows.

### Step 3: Review before finalizing

- Scan for outliers (e.g., accidentally entered 500 instead of 50).
- Confirm absent learners are handled per school policy.

## 5. View performance and reports

### Step 1: Learner performance

- Open a learner profile to view performance history (where enabled).
- Use admission number for confirmation when names are similar.

### Step 2: Class summaries

- Generate class/subject summaries for review meetings.
- Share findings with the academic administrator if needed.

## 6. Data accuracy and security

### Step 1: Avoid common mistakes

- Do not enter marks under the wrong term or assessment type.
- Always confirm admission numbers when selecting a learner.
- Do not share login credentials.

### Step 2: Report issues early

- Report incorrect class lists, missing learners, or wrong subjects to the administrator.

## 7. Getting help (support and escalation)

If you encounter errors, provide the administrator with:

- what you were doing
- the exact error message
- time of occurrence

If possible, also include a screenshot and whether other users are affected.
